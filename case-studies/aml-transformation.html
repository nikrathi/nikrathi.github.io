Case Study

AI-Driven AML Modernization

Reframing AML monitoring as a scalable product platform-improving detection quality, strengthening governance, and reducing operational burden while supporting exam readiness.

Challenge

A high-growth fintech needed to modernize AML monitoring under exam pressure. Workflows were fragmented across tools and teams, alert volumes were high, and audit readiness depended on manual coordination rather than consistent, productized controls.

Approach

  • Defined a product roadmap aligned to regulatory expectations and clear control outcomes (signal quality, governance, throughput, auditability).
  • Integrated analytics into monitoring and triage-pairing decision support with a broad rule stack (80+ AML rules) and standardized workflows.
  • Established governance checkpoints and KPIs to track model/control performance, operational efficiency, and remediation progress.

Execution

  • Led cross-functional squads across compliance, data, engineering, and operations-shipping improvements in phased releases to reduce change risk.
  • Aligned data governance, risk taxonomy, and validation-ready documentation to support auditability and regulator confidence.
  • Operationalized reporting and management routines so leaders could monitor performance and intervene early when thresholds shifted.

Outcomes

  • Improved exam readiness and supported progress through FED/MRA scrutiny with clearer control traceability.
  • Reduced manual effort by ~25% through workflow redesign and productized triage.
  • Reduced false positives (up to ~30%) by integrating analytics with an 80+ rule stack.
  • Created a repeatable product operating model for ongoing enhancements and governance.

Capabilities Demonstrated

  • AML product strategy and roadmap definition
  • Model governance and validation readiness alignment
  • Analytics-enhanced monitoring and triage design
  • Cross-functional delivery across compliance, data, and engineering
  • KPI-driven performance management and remediation tracking
