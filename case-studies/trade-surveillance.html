Case Study

Trade Surveillance Product Transformation Within a Bank

As product lead, I defined and delivered a supervisory trade surveillance dashboard that replaced manual reporting, standardized workflows, and improved issue detection speed for supervisory teams.

Challenge

Trade surveillance reporting within the bank relied on manual spreadsheets and fragmented reviews, creating delayed escalation and low confidence in supervisory visibility. Teams needed one product experience to monitor signals, prioritize risk, and act faster.

Approach

  • Defined product vision, target users, and success metrics for a long-term supervisory platform aligned to compliance and operations goals.
  • Prioritized roadmap outcomes: standardized surveillance KPIs, automated reporting workflows, and drill-down views for exception management.
  • Translated manual supervisory processes into a reusable product model covering thresholds, risk scoring, escalation, and audit trails.

Execution

  • Ran cross-functional delivery across surveillance, compliance, and engineering teams with phased releases and clear product increments.
  • Launched MVP dashboards first, then expanded features for near-real-time monitoring, issue triage, and supervisory decision support.
  • Managed adoption through user playbooks, feedback loops, and KPI reviews to ensure reliability and sustained supervisory usage.

Outcomes

  • Replaced manual supervisory reporting with a productized dashboard used in ongoing trade surveillance oversight.
  • Improved time-to-identification of surveillance issues through standardized signal views and faster escalation pathways.
  • Increased consistency of supervisory reporting while reducing manual workload and operational friction.
  • Established a scalable product foundation for future trade surveillance enhancements and regulator-ready reporting.

Capabilities Demonstrated

  • Product vision and roadmap leadership
  • Trade surveillance dashboard product management
  • Workflow and KPI-driven feature prioritization
  • Cross-functional delivery with compliance and engineering
  • Adoption, governance, and continuous product improvement
